Why is everyone leaving Scooter Braun? Reason behind losing his clients

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, August 21, 2024

Scooter Braun, the prominent American entrepreneur, music executive, and owner of SB Projects, has managed some of the world’s biggest music stars, including Justin Bieber, Ye (formerly Kanye West), and Carly Rae Jepsen. However, recent reports suggest that Braun is facing challenges, as two of his long-term clients, Demi Lovato and Ariana Grande, have reportedly severed ties with him.

Demi Lovato, who had been under Braun’s management since 2019, and Ariana Grande, who had a professional relationship with Braun since 2013, have not publicly disclosed the reasons for their departure. This silence has sparked speculation among fans and the media about potential underlying issues.

While no official explanations have been given, some speculate that Braun might be involved in a yet-to-be-disclosed scandal, leading to the decisions made by Lovato and Grande. A prediction even surfaced on an online platform that intriguing news about Braun could emerge in the near future.

Braun’s SB Projects has also lost other high-profile artists recently, including J Balvin, which has raised questions about the manager’s current standing in the music industry. Some observers are tracing these departures back to a well-known conflict between Braun and Taylor Swift in 2019. The disagreement revolved around Braun’s refusal to allow Swift to regain control of her music catalog from Big Machine Records, leading Swift to re-record much of her music and engage in legal battles.

Ariana Grande’s departure from Braun’s management coincides with the re-release of her debut album, “Yours Truly,” a project that Braun had initially helped her bring to fruition. While the exact reasons for her decision remain undisclosed, it has undoubtedly piqued curiosity among fans.

Even Demi Lovato, who previously defended Braun against accusations from Taylor Swift, has chosen to part ways with him without providing specific details about the decision. As Lovato joined Braun’s roster in 2019, her departure adds to the mystery surrounding the current state of his management company.

As the news of these departures circulates, there have been recent rumors, originating from sources like Puck News, suggesting that Justin Bieber, another significant client of Braun’s, might also consider leaving SB Projects. However, representatives for Bieber have refuted these claims.

Braun’s influence on the music industry is undeniable, especially in Justin Bieber’s case. He played a pivotal role in Bieber’s rise to global stardom, as he and his team strategically built Bieber’s YouTube presence before even securing a record deal. Braun’s dedication to “Project Justin Bieber,” as he referred to it in an interview, highlights his foresight in harnessing the power of the internet for emerging artists.

While the exact reasons behind the recent departures remain undisclosed, the music industry and fans alike are watching closely for any further developments in the evolving narrative of Scooter Braun and his clientele.
