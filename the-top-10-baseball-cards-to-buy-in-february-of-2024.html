The Top 10 Baseball Cards to Buy in February of 2024

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Friday, September 27, 2024

Investing in baseball cards can be a profitable venture, especially when targeting cards with potential for increased value. As of February 2024, several baseball cards are gaining attention from collectors and investors. Here are the top 10 baseball cards that you should consider adding to your collection or investment portfolio.

1. Derek Jeter 1993 Topps #98 Base Raw

  • With 60 sales, the Derek Jeter 1993 Topps #98 Base Raw card is a sought-after piece in the baseball card market. Its popularity and sales volume make it a favorable choice for investment. 

2. Shohei Ohtani 2018 Bowman #49 Base Raw

  • The Shohei Ohtani 2018 Bowman #49 Base Raw card has garnered 51 sales, indicating its strong demand among collectors. Its significance in the market makes it a worthy inclusion in your collection. 

3. Shohei Ohtani 2018 Topps #700 Base (Pitching) Raw

  • Another impactful card, the Shohei Ohtani 2018 Topps #700 Base (Pitching) Raw, shares the same sales count of 51 as the Bowman card, solidifying its position as a top choice for investment. 

4. Ken Griffey Jr. 1989 Donruss #33 Base Raw

  • The Ken Griffey Jr. 1989 Donruss #33 Base Raw card, with 45 sales, continues to attract attention from collectors. Its historical significance and sales volume make it a compelling option for purchase. 

5. Corbin Carroll 2023 Bowman’s Best #20 Refractor Raw

  • Featuring 43 sales, the Corbin Carroll 2023 Bowman’s Best #20 Refractor Raw card stands out as a popular choice among collectors. Its desirability in the market makes it a valuable addition to any baseball card collection. 

6. Shohei Ohtani 2018 Topps Update #US285 Rookie Debut Raw

  • With 43 sales, the Shohei Ohtani 2018 Topps Update #US285 Rookie Debut Raw card maintains a strong presence in the market. Its consistent sales volume makes it an attractive option for purchase. 

7. Ken Griffey Jr. 1989 Fleer #548 Base PSA 9

  • The Ken Griffey Jr. 1989 Fleer #548 Base PSA 9 card, with 42 sales, holds its ground as a notable card in the baseball card market. Its sales volume and graded status make it an appealing choice for collectors and investors.

8. Ken Griffey Jr. 1989 Fleer #548 Base Raw

  • With 41 sales, the Ken Griffey Jr. 1989 Fleer #548 Base Raw card remains a popular choice among collectors. Its consistent demand and sales volume make it a favorable selection for investment.

9. Barry Bonds 1986 Topps Traded #11T Base Raw

  • The Barry Bonds 1986 Topps Traded #11T Base Raw card, with 40 sales, maintains its appeal in the baseball card market. Its sales volume and historical significance make it an attractive option for potential buyers.

10. Tony Gwynn 1983 Topps #482 Base Raw

  • Featuring 40 sales, the Tony Gwynn 1983 Topps #482 Base Raw card holds its position as a notable choice for collectors and investors. Its sales volume and historical relevance make it a compelling addition to any baseball card collection.

The baseball card market in February 2024 offers a variety of options for collectors and investors seeking to expand their collections or make strategic investments. By staying informed about the latest trends and sales data, collectors and investors can make well-informed decisions when acquiring baseball cards.
