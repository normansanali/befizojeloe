Teen Mom Leah Messer shows off her curves in a swimsuit &amp; parties with friends on Virginia beach

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Thursday, August 1, 2024

TEEN Mom 2 star Leah Messer showed off her curves in a swimsuit.

The 29-year-old confidently flaunted her figure while partying with friends on Virginia Beach.

The MTV star took to Instagram earlier this week to share a series of snaps from a recent trip to Virginia Beach.

The first photo showed Leah– who wore a red swimsuit and white cover-up- posing with three friends, while a second video caught the friends cheersing their drinks.

Following photos showed the group smiling for photos while out to dinner.

Leah continued to flaunt her figure while out to eat, as she stunned in a low cut crop top and black shorts.

Alongside the photos, the Teen Mom 2 star wrote: “Chillin’ with my sea squad celebrating two birthday Queens! ??

“@nstegall_ & @nishaaa06 I hope your birthdays were just as amazing as you both are!

“It was an unforgettable and much needed weekend.”

She concluded: “I LOVE YALL!”

The weekend getaway was likely a fun distraction from her problems at home, as the reality star has been open about her concerns over her 11-year-old daughter Ali’s health.

Leah shares twins Ali and Aleeah with ex Corey Simms, while she’s also the mother to eight-year-old Adalynn with ex Jeremy Calvert.

During a recent episode of Teen Mom 2, Leah sat down with Ali for a heart-to-heart about her concerns for her daughter’s safety without the help of her wheelchair.

In the clip, Leah explained how Ali’s wheelchair has provided her with the “freedom” to move around easily.

Ali then insisted that she would be safe venturing to the park, despite her mother’s concerns.

“It’s not even that far away,” Ali said, before looking her mother in the eye assuring her “I’m not going to fall.”

Leah rebutted as she told the preteen: “My job as a mom is to protect you and keep you safe. I’m always going to worry.”

Ali was diagnosed with Titin Myotonic muscular dystrophy- a rare form of progressive weakness disease- in 2014.
