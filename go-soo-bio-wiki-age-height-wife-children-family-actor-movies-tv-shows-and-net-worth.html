Go Soo Career

Go Soo Actor

When Ko Soo first came to Seoul, he acted on stage, but he lacked funds that time. Since he lacked funds, he chose to put his stage acting on hold for later and finally stopped.

From then on, Ko Soo started presenting his profile photo to various companies. He was first featured in a soft drink TV advertisement, supported by a role as an extra in the 1998 music video “Last Promise” by the band Position.

Ko Soo made his television debut in 1999 through the MBC sitcoms My Funky Family and Jump and the KBS2 drama series Ad Madness. He drew crucial acclaim for his performance in the television drama Piano in 2001, with one review describing him as an “actor with precision”.

Ko Soo made his big-screen debut as a drug crime officer in 2004’s Some, in which he performed his own stunts and was later known as Best New Actor at the Grand Bell Awards. He reunited with Ad Madness co-star Park Ye-jin in the TV series When A Man Loves A Woman in the same year.

In 2005, Ko Soo starred in the revenge drama Green Rose, which was shot on location in China and Korea. He played a simple man who falls in love with a rich woman and gets accused of a crime he did not commit.

Then in the romantic comedy Marrying a Millionaire, Ko Soo played a delivery man who is asked by a TV producer to act like a rich bachelor to attract several women on a televised reality dating show.

Go Soo Military

Ko Soo started his two-year compulsory military service on March 2, 2006, and he was appointed as a Civil Service Personnel in Gangnam District, Seoul. He was discharged on April 25, 2008, and received attention for being an outstanding Public Interest Service Personnel.

Go Soo Comeback

For his first post-army project, Ko Soo shocked fans by taking a stage play. Invited to join the “Best Play Series” by veteran actor Cho Jae-Hyun (Jo was his costar in Piano, and the senior colleague he “most respects”), Ko Soo made his theater debut as the lead actor in The Return of President Eom, which ran from May 23 until August 3, 2008.

In 2009, Ko Soo starred in the dark mystery film White Night, based on the Japanese novel Byakuyakō by Keigo Higashino. Go said he was “completely absorbed by the intriguing storyline.”

Ko Soo then made his television comeback in the melodrama Will It Snow for Christmas?, a tale of rekindled childhood love penned by renowned TV writer Lee Kyung-hee. He described his character as “hurt by love and tries to overcome that pain.”

His next film Haunters, in which he played the only man immune to a psychic’s supernatural powers, was a box office hit in 2010. On May 13, 2011, he signed under the management of Lee Byung-hun known as BH Entertainment. He next played a soldier in the Korean War-set The Front Line, then a grieving firefighter in the 2012 romance drama Love 911.

Afterward, Ko Soo collaborated with the makers of The Chaser for Empire of Gold, a TV series about the power struggle within a rich family living through Korea’s turbulent economy of the 1990s, produced by SBS; in this series, he played an antihero.

Later that year, Ko Soo starred in the melodrama film Way Back Home, playing the devastated husband of a woman wrongfully accused of drug smuggling. He said the reason he chose the film was because he wanted to work with acclaimed actress Jeon Do-Yeon and that through his character he “was able to tackle the challenge of internal and external change”.

In 2014, Ko Soo featured in two short films. Directed by Kang Je-Gyu, Awaiting is about a married couple separated for sixty years by the division of North and South Korea. Awaiting was one of the four short films including Beautiful 2014, an omnibus project that premiered at the 38th Hong Kong International Film Festival.

Meanwhile, Myohyangsangwan, which represented the rendezvous of a South Korean painter and a North Korean waitress in a North Korean restaurant, is a collaboration by contemporary artists Moon Kyung-won and Jeon Joon-ho and combined a theatrical plot, experimental imagery, dance, and performance art.

Ko Soo then starred in the period film The Royal Tailor, in the role of an upstart new designer whose talent and instincts challenge the traditional master artisan of royal attire.

In June 2015, Ko Soo has amicably parted ways with BH Entertainment and joined a new agency founded by his previous manager at BH Entertainment. He made his small-screen comeback after three years in MBC’s 55th-founding anniversary historical drama directed by a famed director Lee Byung-hoon, The Flower in Prison.

In 2017, Ko Soo starred in Lucid Dream, a psychological thriller in which he played a former journalist who attempts to find his kidnapped son using lucid dreaming. The same year, he starred in The Tooth and the Nail, a film based on the 1955 mystery novel by Bill S. Ballinger in which a magician discovers the incinerated teeth and fingernails of his missing butler.

He next starred in the period epic film The Fortress. In 2018, Ko Soo was cast in the medical drama Heart Surgeons as a cardiothoracic surgery resident trying to save his mother in need of a heart transplant.
