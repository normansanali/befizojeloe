Why MMA Legend Jon Jones may retire in 2024

Posted by Martina Birk on Monday, July 22, 2024

Injury and Recovery

Jon Jones recently suffered a pectoral muscle injury, which typically takes between 8 and 12 months to recover from. Despite this setback, Jones remains determined to return to the octagon as soon as he recovers. He aims to recover as quickly as possible and fight Stipe Miocic, showcasing his unwavering determination and desire to continue his career in MMA.

Past Challenges and Controversies

Throughout his career, Jones has faced challenges, including testing controversies and financial disagreements with UFC President Dana White. These issues have led to periods of inactivity, causing speculation about his future in the sport.

Struggle for Fair Treatment

In the past, Jones has been vocal about his desire for fair treatment, particularly in terms of financial compensation. This has led to disagreements with the UFC, raising questions about his willingness to continue competing under current conditions.

UFC's Decision on Stripping the Title

There have been debates within the MMA community about whether the UFC should strip Jones of his heavyweight title due to his injury and potential retirement. Some argue that with interim titleholder Tom Aspinall waiting for his shot, the UFC should consider stripping Jones of the title.

Uncertain Future and Retirement Speculation

Given the combination of injury, past controversies, and demands for fair treatment, there is speculation about Jones possibly retiring in 2024. His recent announcement of retirement and the reasoning behind it, including the need for increased payment, has raised doubts about his future in the sport.

The cumulative impact of injury, past challenges, and ongoing controversies may lead Jon Jones to consider retiring from MMA in 2024. His determination to recover from his injury and continue fighting demonstrates his commitment to the sport, but the uncertainty surrounding his future and his demands for fair treatment suggest that retirement remains a possibility.
