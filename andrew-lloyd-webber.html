Andrew Lloyd Webber Biography

Andrew Webber was born in Kensington, England on March 22, 1948. He was born into a musical family, with his father, composer William Lloyd Webber, and mother, pianist and violinist Jean Johnstone. Andrew’s younger brother is a well-known cellist, and his aunt Viola used to be a stage actress.

While attending Westminster School, Andrew was awarded the prestigious Queen’s Scholarship. He later attended Magdalen College in Oxford to study history. But he soon dropped out and went to the Royal College of Music to study music instead.

Career

Andrew Webber began his career as a child prodigy. He began writing music compositions at the age of nine. He began working with lyricist Tim Rice when he was 17 years old, and they embarked on one of the most successful musical collaborations that lasted 13 years.

During this time, Andrew Webber created some of the most memorable musicals, including Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat, Jesus Christ Superstar, and Evita. Due to artistic differences, Webber and Rice split up after Evita.

Webber began his next project without a lyricist, adapting poet T.S. Elliot’s collection of poems into the musical Cats. This show ran on Broadway for 18 years and won numerous awards.

Webber’s next musical hit was The Phantom of the Opera, which was based on a French novel. This musical broke previous records set by Cats and went on to become Broadway’s longest-running musical show.

Andrew Webber has since written, directed, and composed scores of musical shows, the majority of which have been commercially successful.

Andrew Lloyd Webber Achievements and Awards

Andrew Webber’s career has been littered with honors. He has received numerous honors, titles, and awards and is widely regarded as one of the most talented composers of all time.

He has received three Grammy Awards, seven Tony Awards (for outstanding achievement in Broadway theater), and an Academy and Golden Globe Award. He is a member of the Hollywood Walk of Fame and the Songwriters Hall of Fame. The Queen has also awarded Andrew Webber a knighthood and a peerage.

Andrew Lloyd Webber’s Net Worth in 2023

Andrew Lloyd Webber has amassed a net worth of approximately $1.5 billion as of November 2023, making him one of the most commercially successful musical composers. He has mostly made money from the production of his numerous musical compositions, which have done quite well financially.

The Phantom, one of his most successful shows, has earned nearly $5.6 billion worldwide. Andrew Webber also owns all of his musical works through his production company, the Really Useful Group. Andrew Webber, the company’s sole owner, recently received a dividend of nearly $2.7 million.

Andrew Webber is one of the most well-known composers. He is a man who knew exactly what he wanted and worked hard to get it. Despite being at the center of numerous controversies and plagiarism accusations, he has let his music speak for itself. Whatever his detractors say, his musicals’ phenomenal success has silenced them all. He is still the reigning Lord of Music and Melody.

Andrew Lloyd Webber Wife, Marriage

Andrew Webber has had three marriages. His first marriage, to Sarah Hugill, lasted 12 years and produced two children. Andrew married Sarah Brightman, the actress from his musical show Cats, after his divorce from Sarah. He later cast her as the lead actress in his well-known musical The Phantom of the Opera. In 1990, they divorced. Andrew Webber currently resides in Hampshire with his third wife, Madeleine Gurdon, and their three children.
